# Personalization: Course creator / educator

## Top 3 connector recommendations (ranked)

1. **Perplexity Ask** — for trend research, audience-pain validation, sales-page copy mining
2. **VidIQ** — for competitor course teardowns (most courses promote on YouTube)
3. **Firecrawl** — for scraping competitor sales pages, pricing tiers, bonus stacks

## Phase 6 demo (first research live-fire)

Run `/research-brief` on a competing course in the user's niche. Output: brief covering price, positioning, modules, bonuses, and what's missing the user could add.

## Conversation tone for the wizard

Course creators think in students, modules, and outcomes. Use language like "your students," "the transformation you teach," "your launch." Avoid pure-coach or pure-creator jargon.

## Common pitfalls

- Course creators do "competitive research" by reading sales pages with their own bias. The wizard's brief format forces structured comparison.
- Course creators over-research at launch and under-research during quiet periods. Phase 7 cadence (monthly research review) is critical.

## Sample brief topics to suggest in Phase 6

- "Compare [competitor course] to mine — price, modules, bonuses"
- "What's missing in [niche] that I could teach?"
- "Pull the top YouTube videos in [niche] and what they don't cover"

**Quick-capture habit (post-onboarding):** Use `/save-clip` whenever you find an article worth keeping. The library compounds over weeks.
